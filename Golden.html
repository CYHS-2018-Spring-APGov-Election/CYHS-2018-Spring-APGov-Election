MEET TREVOR V. BLOOD

Activist, Government Official (2000 - )

Trevor V. Blood was born in York, Pennsylvania. He elected to run the Golden Party in early March 2017 to begin the rise of a "Golden Age." In early years, he began his education at Central York School District, where he invested many hours and time into various clubs and organizations. He was and is a very involved person within his community with National Honor Society and the International Thespian Society. He worked as a Guest Service Ambassador at Target of East York. In his college years, he attended American University to study Politics and United States Government, where he met his running mate for Vice-President, Alexander Forry.

They founded the Golden Party with the help of Mr. Brent Forry, and Mr. Clayton Brosend. After graduating from American University, he attended New York University for his doctorate, further pursuing his love for politics. He even minored again in French language to be an ambassador to France. In later year, he grew more passionate in politics, and he began protesting social issues that inhibits people the rights that they equally deserved. He attended marches for women and was an activist in the Not One More and the Me Too movements. He works for equality for all, as the whole basis of the Golden Party is to equally represent everyone, despite the background of any person.

Political Stances

GUN REFORM

Due to recent events such as the school shooting in Parkland change needs to happen with our current gun laws. The reform that we would like to see is more restrictive background checks to screen for mental health issues as well as making sure that all the proper information is entered in the system. We would also like to see the required age to buy any kind of firearm be raised to 21. Lastly, we would like to see a gun tax applied to all firearm sales This would keep all gun restrictions and sales within the government so that they can be appropriately regulated.


INTERNATIONAL IMPORTS

In recent years America has been importing more than it exports. This is a major problem because we are sending more money and jobs to other countries because we can get their products cheaper. To combat this we would like to increase import tariffs to put an emphasis on buying American goods. The increase would help to make prices of American products more competitive in our economy. This increase in American made goods will help to boost our economy to new heights.


ABORTION

Only about 20 states have made it legal under certain precautions for women to abort. The other 30 states have many laws that inhibit women the right to choose whether or not they can have this baby. We want more states to have choices for the women. After all, it should be up to the women to decide what happens. People who are not carrying this child should not have any say in what a woman does with her body. A variety of option should be easily obtainable for women, as you truly don't know what goes on in someone's life.


DOMESTICATION OF JOBS

As globalization continues to progress, more and more jobs are being outsourced to different countries. This is because of cheaper labor and the lack tariffs. To bring jobs back to the American people we want to give corporations that operate in America a slight tax break. This break should help to entice them to keep their jobs and manufacturing in the United States. This will definitely improve our unemployment rate which, in turn, will help reduce strain on government spending associated with government assitance and relief programs.


IMMIGRATION

The immigration issue in this country has been around for a long time but has recently become a hot button topic. This issue cannot be ignored. This ideology is firm on the idea that illegal immigration should immediately be shut down. Illegal aliens convicted of felonies or other serious crimes should face mandatory deportation. Additionally, sanctuary cities will lose federal support for their failure to comply with the laws of the country. After passing a comprehensive background check, new immigrants will be allowed to apply 4 immediate family members per year for American citizenship.
